Netherrack is a frail stone block found abundantly in the Nether and in blobs below ruined portals. Although it itself is not flammable, fire lit on top of it burns indefinitely.

Netherrack can be obtained only when mined with a pickaxe. It drops nothing if mined without one.

Netherrack can also be obtained by mining nylium with a pickaxe not enchanted with Silk Touch.

Netherrack makes up most of the Nether's terrain, and also generates as part of ruined portals in the Overworld.
Netherrack generates as part of the bridge and treasure room bastion remnants.

In Bedrock Edition, netherrack generates as part of nether portals spawned floating. This is what makes netherrack renewable.
Crimson or warped nylium transforms into netherrack if a solid, opaque block is directly above it, or when it is covered by snow.

Netherrack is primarily used as a cost-effective building material. Due to its abundance and softness, it is often used to construct temporary structures that serve as scaffolding, blocking, or for other purposes.
When netherrack's top is lit on fire, the fire burns indefinitely. Unlike soul fire, this fire can spread.
Using bone meal on netherrack placed next to warped nylium or crimson nylium transforms the netherrack into more nylium.
Analysing the netherrack in a material reducer, it turns out to be a volcanic silicate rock rich in mercury.

Netherrack can be placed under note blocks to produce "bass drum" sounds.
